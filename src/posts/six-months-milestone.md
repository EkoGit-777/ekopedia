# :star2: Six Months, First Client, and a Hard Lesson

I signed my contract with the company in October—on my birthday, exactly. That makes this month my six-month mark. Looking back, I realize how much has happened, so I consider this a small milestone in my journey here.

On the second week, my Director came to Indonesia again. He was planning to meet one of our clients. On the third day of his visit, we had lunch together with the whole team. We were celebrating our first client—thanks to the great work from the Account Managers.

During that lunch, he shared a bigger vision. Once [Prestia](https://prestia.cloud) is adopted by more schools around Jakarta, the next target will be Bali. And when that time comes, we’ll go there together. It sounded simple, but it gave me something to look forward to.

![](https://cdn.ekopedia.id/images/post/picture-after-lunch.webp)

---

Last week, something unexpected happened—my Instagram and Discord accounts got hacked.

I’m still not entirely sure where the malware came from, but my best guess is an app I downloaded two days earlier. I installed it on my laptop, and that might have been the entry point.

Fortunately, the damage was limited. The attacker only sent spam messages and posted crypto-related stories. After I regained access, I cleaned everything up and explained the situation to people who reached out.

At first, I received a notification from LinkedIn about a suspicious login attempt. Not long after, someone also tried to access my Facebook account. I thought it stopped there.

But in the middle of the night, I woke up to messages saying my Instagram had been hacked. At that point, I assumed it was only targeting my Meta accounts, so I quickly enabled 2FA on Instagram and Threads.

The next morning, I found out my Discord account had also been compromised—and the spam pattern was exactly the same. That’s when everything clicked. The attempts on LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, and Discord were all connected.

I immediately ran a full scan on my laptop and even installed [Malwarebytes](https://www.malwarebytes.com/home-hl) to double-check. The results showed no threats, but I’m still not fully convinced. To be safe, I’m planning to reinstall Windows.

---

Overall, this month gave me a mix of progress and lessons.

On one side, the company reached an important milestone—getting its first client. That means the product I’ve been building has started to prove its value. It’s a small step, but it feels meaningful.

On the other side, the security incident reminded me how fragile things can be if I’m not careful—like soscial media accounts without 2FA.

I’m grateful to be at this point. The company has a supportive environment, a comfortable office, an understanding leader, and a solid team. I hope I can continue growing here for a long time.
